Catching

When catching the ball in rugby, you want to avoid getting a knock-on (see Infringements for more about knock-ons). Therefore, you should not catch the ball straight on because if you miss the ball, it may bounce off your body and land in front of you. Note: if the ball bounces off you and lands behind you, it is not considered a knock-on.

When receiving a pass from a teammate, you should be positioned behind him/her, facing down the field. Ideally, you will receive the ball while running so it is not always possible to catch the ball with your outside foot forward, but it is usually more comfortable for players to catch the ball in this way. You should also have your hands out in front of you, ready to receive the pass. Keep your eyes on the ball until you have possession and control of it. Catch the ball with your fingers and then bring it in toward your body. Once you catch the ball, cradle it with one end tucked in the inside of your elbow, and the other end in your hand.

In order to catch a high ball (e.g. from a kick off), turn slightly to the side so that you are not perpendicular to the ball. Keep your eye on the ball and hold your arms out, reaching up toward the ball. Your opponents will most likely be running straight at you, but do not let this intimidate you. Focus on the ball. When you feel the ball in your arms, bend your knees, let your body give to absorb the shock, and immediately bring the ball to your chest. It is important to catch these high balls properly the first time because when a rugby ball hits the ground from this height, the bounce is somewhat unpredictable. You do not want to be chasing the ball around the field, nor do you want the opposing team to get to the ball first.

Sometimes it is not always possible to catch a ball before it hits the ground (e.g. from a grubber kick). In order to catch a bouncing rugby ball, hold your hands out, stay on the balls of your feet, and keep your eye on the ball. It is best to wait until the ball stops rolling quickly and low to the ground. Most times, the ball will slow down and bounce higher up into the air. It is easier to avoid getting a knock-on by catching it at this high point. When you feel the ball in your hands or arms, bring it to your chest and control it.
