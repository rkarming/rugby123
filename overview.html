Game Overview

A rugby match is played on a rugby field, or pitch, between two teams of 15 players each. A match is at least 80 minutes long and is comprised of two 40-minute halves with a maximum of a 10-minute break in-between each half. The objective of the game is to advance the ball to the opposing team's side of the field to score points. However, the ball cannot be passed forward, only laterally or backward, so players must gain ground by running with the ball or kicking it. The team that scores the most points by the end of the game wins the match.

The Pitch


Rugby pitch playing enclosure. The field of play is outlined in blue.

There are several different lines and areas on a rugby pitch. The most important lines are the half way line, 10 meter lines, 22 meter lines, goal lines, dead ball lines, touchlines, and touch-in-goal lines, each of which is labeled on the diagram. The areas of a rugby pitch are as follows:

Field of Play
the area between the two goal lines and the two touch lines; it does not include the goal lines or the touchlines. The field of play is 100 meters long by 70 meters wide.
In-Goal Area
the area between the goal line, dead ball line, and two touch-in-goal lines; it includes the goal line, but not the dead ball line or two touch-in-goal lines. There are two in-goal areas on the pitch, one behind each goal post. Each in-goal area is 10 meters long by 70 meters wide.
Playing Area
the area that includes the field of play and the in-goal areas; it does not include the touchlines, touch-in-goal lines, or dead ball lines. The playing area is 120 meters long by 70 meters wide.
Perimeter Area
the space around the outside of the playing area. The perimeter area should be no less than 5 meters wide, where possible, to keep spectators and other players away from the playing area.
Playing Enclosure
the area that includes the perimeter area and the playing area. The playing enclosure should be no less than 130 meters long by 80 meters wide.

A rugby pitch has two goal posts, one on each goal line. Each goal post is 5.6 meters wide and has a minimum height of 3.4 meters. Finally, each goal post has a horizontal crossbar, which is 3 meters high, measuring from the ground to the top edge of the crossbar.

Note: The pitch setup above is for collegiate and club (adult) rugby. USA Rugby provides guidance on recommended dimensions for the playing area and recommended maximums for times of play, for youth and high school rugby.

Positions

This diagram illustrates the positions of a 15-player rugby team. During a match, these positions are mirrored by the opposing team on the opposite side of the pitch.

Rugby union has 15 players per team and every person plays offense and defense. Each jersey number corresponds to the position played. Numbers 1 through 8 are forwards and numbers 9 through 15 are backs. Any jersey number above 15 signifies a substitute player. The numbers and positions are as follows:

  1. Loose head prop
  2. Hooker
  3. Tight head prop
  4. Lock
  5. Lock
  6. Blindside flanker
  7. Openside flanker
  8. Number eight
  9. Scrumhalf
  10. Flyhalf
  11. Left wing
  12. Inside center
  13. Outside center
  14. Right wing
  15. Full back

The forwards usually consist of the bigger, heavier, and stronger players. Collectively, these eight players are referred to as the pack. The forwards' primary job is to win possession of the ball. On the other hand, the backs usually consist of the smaller, faster, more elusive players. Their primary job is to take the ball won by the forwards and move it quickly down the field.

Rugby 7s


This diagram illustrates the positions of a rugby 7s team. During a match, these positions are mirrored by the opposing team on the opposite side of the pitch.

Rugby 7s (or rugby sevens) is a variation of 15-player rugby union, in which seven players per team play two seven-minute halves. Each team is composed of three forwards and four backs. The numbers and positions are as follows:

  1. Prop
  2. Hooker
  3. Prop
  4. Scrumhalf
  5. Flyhalf
  6. Center
  7. Wing

Below is a summary of some of the primary variations between rugby union and rugby 7s.

Rugby Union v. Rugby 7s
Rugby Union Rugby 7s
Players per team 15 (8 forwards, 7 backs) 7 (3 forwards, 4 backs)
Game time 40-minute halves, 10-minute halftime 7-minute halves, 2-minute halftime
Substitutes 7 substitutes, 7 interchanges 5 substitutes, 5 interchanges
Conversion kick type Drop kick or place kick Drop kick
Conversion time limit Attempt must be made within 90 seconds of scoring try Attempt must be made within 30 seconds of scoring try
Scrum 8-player scrum 3-player scrum
Restart kick Opponent of scoring team takes restart kick Scoring team takes restart kick
Yellow card 10-minute suspension 2-minute suspension
View more details about 7s laws.

Rugby 7s is administered by World Rugby, the international governing body for union rugby.
